##**Conclusion:**

For every field I know, I know a whole bunch of stuff I don't know, but for those fields I don't know, I can't say what I don't know.

Sometimes I feel that I am a little more confident when my technical quarters are lacking, maybe because at that time, the boundary of knowledge I can perceive is only as big as 2^4 = 16, and I feel that knowing 2^2=4 is enough Yes, after all, it's a quarter of the time, and I think I'm pretty good~!

In the end, when my "knowledge" really grew to 16, I realized "Oh, so there are so many things in computer science"? Moreover, the water in each field is very deep. The textbooks cite papers, and the papers cite more papers, which are constantly fractal like a tree... The cognitive "boundary" has also grown to 2^16 = 65536. The things I know only account for 0.02%, and I feel that I am really nothing. I call this nonsense the "exponential growth theory of cognition."

And all I can do is learn to live peacefully with that perception.
